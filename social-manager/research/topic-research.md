Find trending topics, news, and conversation starters across your content categories to fuel authentic, timely posts.

## Research Process

### 1. Identify Topic Area
Determine which category to research based on:
- User request
- Content calendar needs
- Topic distribution frequency
- Recent posting history

### 2. Execute Search Queries

**Trending news:**
```
"[topic] news [this week/month]"
"latest [topic] developments"
"[topic] controversy"
"[topic] breakthrough"
```

**Industry discussions:**
```
"[topic] debate"
"[topic] vs [alternative]"
"[topic] problem"
"[topic] best practices"
```

**Tool/product launches:**
```
"new [topic] tool"
"[topic] product launch"
"[topic] startup"
"[topic] release"
```

**Community conversations:**
```
site:reddit.com [topic]
site:news.ycombinator.com [topic]
site:twitter.com [topic]
site:linkedin.com [topic]
```

### 3. Evaluate Relevance

**Good topics:**
- Aligns with expertise
- Has an angle you can add value to
- Recent (within 1-2 weeks)
- Generates conversation
- Relates to target audience

**Skip:**
- Too niche (unless super relevant)
- Pure news (unless you have insight)
- Overdone (everyone's posting about it)
- Off-brand or controversial

### 4. Generate Content Angles

Transform research into posts by:
- Adding personal experience
- Providing contrarian view
- Breaking down complexity
- Sharing practical application
- Connecting to audience pain points

## Research Workflows

### Daily Quick Research
Find 1-2 fresh topics for immediate posting.

**Process:**
1. Search latest news in primary categories
2. Scan top 5 results
3. Pick most interesting
4. Generate post idea
5. Create content

**Time:** 10-15 minutes

### Weekly Deep Research
Build content bank for the week ahead.

**Process:**
1. Search all categories
2. Collect 10-15 potential topics
3. Rank by relevance and engagement potential
4. Draft rough post ideas
5. Schedule throughout week

**Time:** 30-45 minutes

### Monthly Trend Analysis
Identify longer-term themes and directions.

**Process:**
1. Search monthly trends in each category
2. Identify patterns and shifts
3. Plan content themes
4. Create monthly calendar
5. Schedule posts

**Time:** 1-2 hours

## Content Angle Framework

### Personal Experience
```
Research: [Topic/trend]
Angle: "I tried [thing] for [time]. Here's what happened."
Value: First-hand insight
```

### Contrarian Take
```
Research: [Popular opinion]
Angle: "Everyone says X. Here's why they're wrong."
Value: Fresh perspective
```

### Simplification
```
Research: [Complex topic]
Angle: "[Topic] explained in [simple terms/metaphor]"
Value: Accessibility
```

### Practical Application
```
Research: [Theory/concept]
Angle: "How to actually use [thing] in [real scenario]"
Value: Actionable advice
```

### Problem-Solution
```
Research: [Common pain point]
Angle: "[Problem] is broken. Here's how to fix it."
Value: Solutions
```

## Source Evaluation

**Trustworthy sources:**
- Official announcements
- Industry publications
- Academic research
- Reputable tech blogs
- First-hand accounts

**Verify before using:**
- Social media rumors
- Unverified claims
- Clickbait headlines
- Anonymous sources
- Extremely recent (< 24 hours)

**Always:**
- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Check publication date
- Consider bias
- Verify facts
- Link to source when relevant

## Content Idea Generator

When research yields a good topic, generate 3-5 angle variations:

**Example:**
Topic: "New AI image generator released"

**Angles:**
1. Quick review post
2. Comparison with existing tools
3. Practical use case tutorial
4. Opinion on implications for designers
5. Thread on AI art ethics

Pick the angle that:
- Matches your expertise
- Fits platform (X vs LinkedIn vs YouTube)
- Adds most value
- Generates best engagement

## Red Flags to Avoid

**Don't post about:**
- Breaking news without verification
- Highly controversial topics (unless necessary)
- Topics outside expertise
- Oversaturated trends
- Purely promotional content
- Misleading headlines

**When in doubt:**
- Wait 24 hours
- Research deeper
- Get second opinion
- Skip it
